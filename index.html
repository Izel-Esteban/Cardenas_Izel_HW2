Hughie Campbell

Hughie is the central character through which readers or viewers can relate to the dark and twisted world of superheroes in 'The Boys.' Throughout the story, he goes through a journey of self-discovery and revenge while facing both physical and emotional challenges. He is known for his reluctant nature and sense of justice, making him a complex and charismatic character in the plot.

In the television adaptation, the character of Hughie is portrayed by actor Jack Quaid, which added a new dimension to the character and made him beloved by fans of the series. In summary, Hughie is a crucial character in 'The Boys,' embodying the fight against the corruption of superheroes and the quest for justice in a world filled with supernatural powers and dark secrets.

Mother's Milk

Mother's Milk, whose real name is Marvin T. Milk, is a key member of "The Boys," a covert group dedicated to exposing and controlling corrupt superheroes. He is known for his loyalty, intelligence, and strong moral compass. Mother's Milk possesses superhuman strength and durability, which makes him a formidable asset to the team.

M.M. is often the voice of reason within the group, offering a grounded perspective in the chaotic world of super-powered individuals. His nickname, "Mother's Milk," is derived from his commitment to a promise he made to his mother to avoid drugs, even though Compound V (the substance that grants superpowers) is in his system, which is a testament to his strong sense of responsibility and ethics.

Billy Butcher

Billy Butcher, often simply referred to as "Butcher," is the uncompromising and ruthless leader of "The Boys," a clandestine group dedicated to exposing and controlling corrupt superheroes. He is known for his unwavering determination and unrelenting pursuit of justice, even if it means using extreme and violent methods to achieve his goals.

Butcher is depicted as a morally ambiguous character who will stop at nothing to achieve his mission, making him both a formidable adversary and a conflicted antihero. His character explores themes of vengeance, manipulation, and the fine line between heroism and villainy in a world filled with superpowered individuals. Actor Karl Urban portrayed Butcher in the television adaptation, delivering a memorable performance that brought the character to life on screen.

Frenchie

Frenchie, known by his nickname, is a member of "The Boys," a covert team dedicated to confronting and controlling corrupt superheroes. He is a highly skilled and resourceful individual with expertise in weaponry, explosives, and various other technical skills. Frenchie is known for his eccentric personality, which includes a love for art and a somewhat quirky demeanor.

While Frenchie may come across as impulsive and chaotic, he is a valuable asset to the team, using his knowledge of explosives and technology to counter the superpowered abilities of the heroes. He is also characterized by his loyalty and deep affection for his fellow teammates, particularly The Female, with whom he shares a special connection and protective nature.

Kimiko-

Kimiko, also referred to as "The Female," is a superpowered individual with a tragic and mysterious past. She is a member of "The Boys" and plays a significant role in the team's efforts to expose and control corrupt superheroes. Kimiko possesses superhuman strength, agility, and regenerative abilities, making her a formidable combatant.

Her character is introduced as a mute, traumatized, and initially violent individual who has suffered at the hands of superheroes. She communicates primarily through sign language, and her backstory is slowly unveiled throughout the series, revealing the horrific experiences she has endured.
